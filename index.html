Common Questions

Everything you're afraid
to ask — answered.

We know chiropractic is new to many Nigerians. Here are the most common questions we hear every day.

Is chiropractic adjustment painful?
+
Most patients feel immediate relief after an adjustment — not pain. You may hear a popping sound, which is simply gas releasing from the joint, not bones cracking. Some patients feel mild soreness for 24 hours after their first session, similar to post-exercise soreness. Dr. Paul always adjusts gently and explains every step before he does it.
How many sessions will I need?
+
It depends on your condition. Many patients feel significant relief after just 1–3 sessions. For chronic conditions like scoliosis or long-term back pain, a course of 6–12 sessions is common. Dr. Paul will assess your condition at the initial consultation and give you a clear, honest treatment plan with realistic expectations.
Is chiropractic care safe?
+
Yes — chiropractic is one of the safest forms of healthcare for musculoskeletal conditions. Dr. Paul is fully trained and qualified. Serious complications are extremely rare — far rarer than the risks of long-term painkiller use or unnecessary surgery. He will conduct a thorough assessment before any treatment to ensure it is safe for you.
What happens during my first visit?
+
Your first visit is a full consultation — about 60 minutes. Dr. Paul will take a detailed history of your pain, assess your posture and spine, perform specific tests, and explain exactly what he finds. If treatment is appropriate that same day, he may begin. You will leave with a clear diagnosis and personalised treatment plan.
Do I need a referral from a doctor?
+
No referral is needed. You can book directly with us online, by phone, or via WhatsApp. Chiropractic is a primary healthcare profession — you can see Dr. Paul as your first point of contact for back pain, neck pain, headaches, and many other conditions without seeing a GP first.
Can children and elderly people receive chiropractic care?
+
Absolutely. Chiropractic care is suitable for all ages — from newborns to the elderly. Techniques are always adapted to the patient. Gentle, low-force adjustments are used for children and older patients. Many parents bring their children for posture checks, scoliosis screening, and sports injury treatment.
Is chiropractic care safe during pregnancy?
+
Yes — and it is highly recommended. Pregnancy places enormous stress on the lower back and pelvis. Dr. Paul uses specially modified, gentle techniques that are completely safe for mother and baby throughout all trimesters. Many women experience dramatic relief from pregnancy-related back pain and pelvic discomfort.
How is chiropractic different from physiotherapy?
+
Chiropractic focuses primarily on spinal alignment and the nervous system — correcting the structural root cause of pain through manual adjustments. Physiotherapy focuses more on rehabilitation, strengthening exercises, and restoring movement after injury. At WhiteRock we offer both, and Dr. Paul often combines approaches for the best results.
What should I wear to my appointment?
+
Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing. You do not need to wear anything specific — Dr. Paul will ask you to remove shoes and may ask you to change into a gown if needed for proper assessment. Leave tight jeans and formal clothing at home if possible.
Once I start, do I have to keep coming forever?
+
No — this is a common myth. Treatment has a clear beginning, middle, and end. Once your condition is resolved, Dr. Paul may recommend periodic wellness visits (once a month or every few months) to maintain your results, but this is entirely your choice. Think of it like servicing your car — optional but beneficial.
