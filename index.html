Common Questions

Everything you're afraid
to ask — answered.

We know chiropractic is new to many Nigerians. Here are the most common questions we hear every day.

Is chiropractic adjustment painful?
+
Most patients feel immediate relief after an adjustment — not pain. You may hear a popping sound, which is simply gas releasing from the joint. Some patients feel mild soreness for 24 hours after their first session, similar to post-exercise soreness.
How many sessions will I need?
+
It depends on your condition. Many patients feel significant relief after just 1–3 sessions. For chronic conditions like scoliosis or long-term back pain, a course of 6–12 sessions is common. Dr. Paul will give you a clear, honest treatment plan at your initial consultation.
Is chiropractic care safe?
+
Yes — chiropractic is one of the safest forms of healthcare for musculoskeletal conditions. Dr. Paul is fully trained and qualified. Serious complications are extremely rare — far rarer than the risks of long-term painkiller use or unnecessary surgery.
What happens during my first visit?
+
Your first visit is a full consultation — about 60 minutes. Dr. Paul will take a detailed history of your pain, assess your posture and spine, perform specific tests, and explain exactly what he finds. You will leave with a clear diagnosis and personalised treatment plan.
Do I need a referral from a doctor?
+
No referral is needed. You can book directly with us online, by phone, or via WhatsApp. Chiropractic is a primary healthcare profession — you can see Dr. Paul as your first point of contact for back pain, neck pain, headaches, and many other conditions.
Can children and elderly people receive chiropractic care?
+
Absolutely. Chiropractic care is suitable for all ages — from newborns to the elderly. Techniques are always adapted to the patient. Gentle, low-force adjustments are used for children and older patients.
Is chiropractic care safe during pregnancy?
+
Yes — and it is highly recommended. Dr. Paul uses specially modified, gentle techniques that are completely safe for mother and baby throughout all trimesters. Many women experience dramatic relief from pregnancy-related back pain and pelvic discomfort.
How is chiropractic different from physiotherapy?
+
Chiropractic focuses primarily on spinal alignment and the nervous system — correcting the structural root cause of pain through manual adjustments. Physiotherapy focuses more on rehabilitation, strengthening exercises, and restoring movement after injury. At WhiteRock we offer both.
What should I wear to my appointment?
+
Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing. You do not need to wear anything specific — Dr. Paul will ask you to remove shoes and may ask you to change into a gown if needed for proper assessment.
Once I start, do I have to keep coming forever?
+
No — this is a common myth. Treatment has a clear beginning, middle, and end. Once your condition is resolved, Dr. Paul may recommend periodic wellness visits to maintain your results, but this is entirely your choice.
